Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

Father of the Nation

Here's a time line of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's life:

  • 1920 - Mujib was born in 1920 into the Bengali Muslim aristocratic Sheikh family of the village of Tungipara in Gopalganj sub-division of Faridpur district in the province of Bengal in British India.
  • 1946 - During the Calcutta riots of 1946, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman worked to maintain communal harmony. After the establishment of Pakistan in 1947, he began to agitate for the rights of Bengalis.
  • 1952 - Sheikh Mujibur Rahman played a key role in the Language Movement of 1952. He led the students and campaigned to establish Bengali as the national language of Pakistan.
  • 1966 - Sheikh Mujibur Rahman launched the Six-Point Movement. The Six Points were a demand for greater autonomy for Bengalis. This movement marked the beginning of the Bengali resistance against the Pakistani rulers.
  • 1969 - He led the 1969 mass uprising. This uprising led to the fall of the Ayub Khan government.
  • 1970 - In the 1970 general elections, the Awami League won an absolute majority. However, Pakistani ruler Zulfikar Ali Bhutto refused to hand over power.
  • 1971 - On March 25, 1971, the Pakistani army carried out a genocide against unarmed Bengalis. In protest of this genocide, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman declared independence. After nine months of a bloody war in 1971, Bangladesh gained independence on December 16. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman became the first president of independent Bangladesh.
  • 1975 - Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was assassinated in a military coup on August 15, 1975. His death was mourned by Bangladesh as a national tragedy.

"Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was not only a father to me but also a father to the nation. I am honored to have been born as his daughter and to have followed in his footsteps to serve the people of Bangladesh. I will continue to strive to uphold his ideals and make his dreams for our nation a reality."

-- Bangladesh's Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina
